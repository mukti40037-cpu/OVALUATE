Actuarial Foundation

AVM Accuracy & Actuarial Requirements

Insurance underwriting from AVM data requires establishing accuracy benchmarks before regulators accept it as a trigger mechanism. This is the critical path item.

Accuracy Benchmarks

Regulator Requirements

  • Median Absolute Percentage Error (MdAPE) <5% for residential
  • Within-10% accuracy rate >80% of all valuations
  • Backtested against minimum 3 years of transaction data
  • Separate accuracy bands by property type and emirate
  • Independent RICS-accredited third-party validation required
Actuarial Build

Loss Model Inputs

  • Minimum 24-month AVM track record before PI underwriting viable
  • Frequency: how often does AVM error exceed 10%?
  • Severity: average financial loss when error threshold breached?

DFSA vs CBUAE — Two Tracks

PRYPCO's existing DFSA licence (F007958) gives a faster path for DIFC products. Onshore UAE products require a parallel CBUAE track. Click each step for detail.

1

DFSA MGA Application — Q3 2026

Apply for Managing General Agent licence under DFSA. PRYPCO's existing DIFC presence and F007958 licence significantly reduces friction. Estimated 3–4 months for approval. Enables Products 1, 5, and 6 (parametric).

2

Reinsurance Treaty — Q3 2026 (Parallel)

Secure capacity agreement with Munich Re or Swiss Re MENA desk. Parametric and PI products require reinsurer pre-approval of trigger methodology before regulatory submission. Aon or Marsh as RI broker.

3

CBUAE Registration — Q4 2026

Separate CBUAE insurance broker/MGA registration for onshore UAE products (Products 2, 3, 4). Longer timeline: 6–9 months. Must run parallel to DFSA track, not sequential. Enables Club Apparel mass-market products.

4

Product Filing & Pricing Approval

Each product requires individual filing with actuarial pricing justification. DFSA is principles-based (faster, ~2 weeks/product). CBUAE requires more prescriptive actuarial documentation (~4–6 weeks/product). Budget accordingly.
